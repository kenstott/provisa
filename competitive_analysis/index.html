    Query Optimization Exceeds Neo4j, Apollo, Hasura. No Cost-Based Optimizer — Provisa trades CBO breadth for targeted high-value optimizations: hot table value CTEs (frequently referenced lookup values pre-materialized and injected automatically), automated MV rewrite (transparent join pattern matching), manual MV optimization (steward-controlled with scheduled refresh). The key structural advantage: a relationship declaration is simultaneously a governance artifact and an optimization hint. The join shape defined by an approved relationship — two tables, two columns, a join type — is exactly what the MV optimizer needs. Cross-source relationships automatically generate pre-materialized join tables at startup; same-source relationships can opt in. No separate optimization config is required. Stewards who think through and approve valid relationships get query acceleration as a direct byproduct. For workloads dominated by repeated lookup patterns and known join shapes, these optimizations often deliver more practical gain than a general-purpose CBO. Starburst exceeds Provisa on raw CBO depth for complex ad-hoc analytical queries. Mature Cypher query planner with cost-based optimization, index-backed lookups, and execution plan tuning — likely exceeds Provisa for native graph query optimization.
